## Summary

This source aggregates IBM's unified practice, guidelines, and ethics for designing artificial intelligence systems. It establishes that designing for AI requires building a collaborative, trust-based relationship between humans and machines. The documentation spans technical fundamentals (rules-based vs. example-based models), relational development stages, five Everyday Ethics focal areas, and conversation design principles (Minimum Viable Knowledge and bot personality).

## Extracted Content

The IBM framework is structured around four primary pillars:

1. **AI Design Foundations & Symbiosis** (File 1):
   - Design lenses: **Purpose** (evolving engagement), **Value** (augmented capabilities), and **Trust** (security, human control, quality).
   - Characterized by four traits: AI *Understands* context, *Reasons* toward goals, *Learns* continuously, and *Interacts* naturally.
   - Applies *Knapp's Relational Development Model* to human-AI interaction (Initiating, Experimenting, Intensifying, Integrating, Bonding).
   - Maps the *AI/Human Context Model*: Intent, Data/Policy, Understanding, Reasoning, Knowledge, Expression, User Reaction, Learning, and Outcome.

2. **AI Basics & Cognitive Methods** (File 2):
   - AI is categorized into *Artificial General Intelligence* (AGI - aspirational) and *Artificial Narrow Intelligence* (ANI - weak AI).
   - Methods: **Rules-based** (algorithms/deterministic instructions) vs. **Example-based** (training machine learning models on structured/unstructured data).
   - Lists 6 core intents: Accelerate research, Enrich interactions, Anticipate disruptions, Recommend with confidence, Scale expertise, Detect liabilities.

3. **Everyday Ethics** (File 3):
   - AI must remain flexible for constant ethical assessment and bias mitigation.
   - Evaluates design through **5 Focal Areas**: *Accountability*, *Value Alignment*, *Explainability*, *Fairness*, and *User Data Rights*.

4. **Conversation Planning** (File 4):
   - Focuses on establishing bot purpose and justifying conversation as the right modality.
   - Introduces **Minimum Viable Knowledge (MVK)**: the minimum set of topics a conversational agent needs to discuss to fulfill its purpose.
   - Emphasizes mapping topic depth, managing error-handling/elegant failures, and designing bot personality traits.
